## Key formulas

<Callout type="key" title="The two calculations">
**Concentration:** mcg/mL = total peptide (mcg) ÷ BAC water added (mL)

**Units to draw:** desired dose (mcg) ÷ concentration (mcg/mL) × 100

**Shortcut:** mcg per unit = concentration ÷ 100
</Callout>

## The whole module in two sentences

Route determines how much of a dose reaches the target and how fast — sub-Q is the default, IM is faster, intranasal bypasses first-pass metabolism, and oral works for select peptides (BPC-157, KPV) when the target is GI tissue rather than systemic circulation. Reconstituting a vial creates a concentration, and every dose is just a division problem: desired dose ÷ concentration = volume in mL, times 100 = units on a U-100 syringe.
