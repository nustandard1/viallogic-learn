## The whole module in one paragraph

Every injury progresses through three phases: inflammatory (0–72h, necessary, coordinates repair signals), proliferative (days–weeks, angiogenesis and fibroblast activity rebuild the tissue), and remodeling (weeks–months, collagen matures from weak Type III to strong Type I). Angiogenesis — driven by VEGF — restores the blood supply that delivers everything repair requires; it's the rate-limiting step in how fast Phase 2 can run. Fibroblasts produce collagen, but collagen maturation takes far longer than pain resolution, which is why re-injury rates stay high even after symptoms clear. Acute inflammation is a necessary phase and shouldn't be aggressively suppressed early; chronic inflammation is the stuck, destructive version that repair peptides are most effective against. BPC-157, TB-500, and GHK-Cu each plug into specific parts of this cascade — the next three compound modules will show exactly where.
