## The module in one paragraph

Most peptide evidence lives at the in vitro or animal model level — but most people encounter it as a confident claim on social media, several distortion steps removed from the original source. The hierarchy runs from cell culture (mechanism only) through animal models (in vivo but not human) to human observational data (uncontrolled) to RCTs (controlled, blinded, randomized). Study quality markers — sample size, blinding, control groups, pre-registration, funding source — determine how much confidence a particular study earns. Statistical significance (p < 0.05) does not equal clinical significance, and publication bias means the visible literature is skewed toward positive findings. When evaluating a claim online, the key questions are: Is a source cited? What type of study is it? Does the claim match what the source actually says? What was the dose? Does this person have a financial incentive? Phrases like "studies show," "clinically proven," and "doctors don't want you to know" are red flags that signal overclaiming. On the product side, a legitimate Certificate of Analysis is produced by an ISO 17025–accredited independent laboratory, contains a lot number matching the vial, specifies the analytical methodology (HPLC for purity, MS for identity), and can be independently verified through the accrediting body's database.
