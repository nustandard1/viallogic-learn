"TB-500" is a vendor name. It's the name the research peptide market gave to a synthetic compound designed to match the sequence of Thymosin Beta-4 — a 43-amino acid peptide with the abbreviation Tβ4. TB-500 is not itself Tβ4. It's a lab-synthesized product that may match the full Tβ4 sequence, or may be a shorter active fragment, depending on the vendor. That distinction matters before anything else.

## Tβ4 is genuinely endogenous — TB-500 is a synthetic analog

This module is largely about Tβ4's biology, because that's what TB-500 is modeled on and what the published research is about. But those two things should be held separately in your mind: **Tβ4** is an endogenous peptide produced by your body; **TB-500** is a synthetic product that may or may not faithfully reproduce Tβ4's sequence, produced in a laboratory.

In the last module, we were careful about the word "endogenous" for BPC-157. BPC-157 is derived from a human gastric protein sequence, but is not itself produced by the body as a free peptide and does not circulate at physiologically active concentrations. The "endogenous" label for BPC-157 refers only to sequence origin — it's imprecise in a way that matters.

Tβ4 is genuinely endogenous in a way BPC-157 is not. It is one of the most abundant peptides in virtually all mammalian cells — particularly platelets, white blood cells, and most tissue types. It's produced by cells, regulated, and released as part of normal biological signaling. The body makes it continuously. Plasma concentrations are measurable and physiologically active. It has characterized biological functions.

What does this mean for TB-500 the product? The endogenous status of Tβ4 informs the risk profile in specific ways:
- Immune recognition: Tβ4 is recognized as "self" — a synthetic product with the same sequence is less likely to trigger an immune response than a genuinely foreign peptide
- Mechanism confidence: Tβ4's biology has been studied for decades as endogenous signaling, giving the mechanism a much stronger research foundation than most research peptides

What it does not mean: that TB-500 carries the same safety assurance as the Tβ4 your body makes. TB-500 is a synthetic product at an administered dose — above-physiological concentrations, at a time of your choosing rather than the body's. That's pharmacology, and pharmacology has effects the natural system wasn't designed for.

## The naming problem

Published research uses "Thymosin Beta-4" or "Tβ4." Almost no primary literature uses "TB-500." When researchers in this space say "the studies show TB-500 works," the studies they're referencing are overwhelmingly Tβ4 studies. This is worth understanding because:

- Searching for "TB-500" in PubMed returns almost nothing
- Searching for "thymosin beta-4 tendon" or "thymosin beta-4 wound healing" returns the relevant literature
- The vendor name TB-500 sometimes refers to the full 43-amino acid sequence, and sometimes to a shorter active fragment — this inconsistency in the research peptide market means you should verify what you're actually getting

<Callout type="info" title="The fragment question">
The most biologically active region of Tβ4 for actin binding is a short internal sequence — roughly residues 17–23 (the peptide Ac-LKKTETQ). Some research has studied this fragment specifically, and some vendors sell it as a distinct product. Most TB-500 products on the research market are the full 43-amino acid Tβ4 sequence or close to it. The full sequence has been studied most extensively. When evaluating evidence claims, confirming which form was studied is the same due diligence as confirming the dose.
</Callout>

## The research landscape

Tβ4 has been in clinical and preclinical research since the 1980s, covering a broader range of conditions than most compounds in this course. The strongest evidence is in two areas:

**Cardiac protection.** In animal models of myocardial infarction, Tβ4 administration produces meaningful reductions in infarct size, improved cardiac function, and promotion of cardiac progenitor cell activation. RegeneRx Biopharmaceuticals conducted Phase 2 trials of Tβ4 for cardiac surgery patients (the REVEAL trial) and for dry eye syndrome. This is a more developed clinical evidence base than most research peptides reach.

**Wound and corneal healing.** Tβ4 accelerates re-epithelialization in skin wound models and corneal wound models. The FDA granted Investigational New Drug status for its use in corneal healing. Multiple controlled animal studies exist across wound types.

**Musculoskeletal.** Animal model evidence exists for tendon and muscle injury — but it is less extensive and less replicated than the cardiac and wound healing literature. This is the application most people in the research peptide space focus on, and it has the thinnest evidence relative to the others. The mechanisms are plausible and the animal findings are positive, but musculoskeletal TB-500 users are extrapolating from better evidence in other tissue types.

<Callout type="key" title="Where the evidence is strongest vs. where it's used most">
TB-500 is most commonly used in the research peptide community for musculoskeletal applications — tendon recovery, soft tissue repair, injury resilience. That is also where the evidence base is weakest relative to other Tβ4 applications. The cardiac and wound healing data is more robust and provides mechanistic confidence. The specific translation to human musculoskeletal outcomes has not been demonstrated in controlled trials.

This is the Module 5 framework: mechanisms that are solid, animal evidence that is real, human clinical confirmation for the specific application that is absent.
</Callout>

<Checkpoint number={1}>
<span slot="question">A colleague says "TB-500 is endogenous so it must be safe — it's just what the body already makes." What's the accurate version of that claim, and where does it go wrong?</span>

There are two accurate parts and one conflation to untangle. First accurate part: Tβ4 — the biological molecule TB-500 is modeled on — is genuinely endogenous. The body produces it, regulates it, and uses it as a normal signaling molecule. Second accurate part: this does meaningfully distinguish TB-500 from many research peptides — immune recognition risk is lower because the sequence is recognized as "self," and the mechanism is well-characterized from decades of endogenous biology research. The conflation: TB-500 is not Tβ4. It's a synthetic product that may match Tβ4's sequence or may be a shorter fragment, depending on the vendor. And even if the sequence is identical, administering it as an external compound at a chosen dose and time is pharmacology — not the body's regulated system restoring itself. "Naturally produced" applies to Tβ4; "what the body already makes" is an imprecise way to describe a synthetic injection. The endogenous status narrows the risk profile in specific ways but does not eliminate pharmacological risk or make dose irrelevant.
</Checkpoint>

The origin story is cleaner than BPC-157's. The mechanism is what earns attention — and it starts with the most abundant protein in your cells.
