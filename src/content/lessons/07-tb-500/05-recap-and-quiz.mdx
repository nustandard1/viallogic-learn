## The module in one paragraph

TB-500 is a synthetic 7-amino acid fragment of Thymosin Beta-4 (Tβ4) — specifically Ac-LKKTETQ, residues 17–23, the actin-binding domain of the full 43-amino acid protein. TB-500 is not itself endogenous as a free peptide; the parent protein Tβ4 is genuinely endogenous, which informs the risk profile but does not transfer to the synthetic fragment directly. TB-500's confirmed mechanism is actin sequestration: Ac-LKKTETQ binds free G-actin monomers in a 1:1 complex, maintaining a buffered pool that enables faster, more coordinated actin polymerization when cells need to migrate. This drives faster wound closure and tissue repair through enhanced cell motility — the one mechanism clearly carried by the fragment. Additional effects attributed to Tβ4 in the literature — stem cell mobilization from bone marrow, NF-κB suppression, cardiac cytoprotection — are documented for full-length Tβ4, not specifically for the fragment; whether the fragment alone produces these effects is plausible but not established. All the clinical and animal model efficacy evidence (cardiac Phase 2 trials, wound healing, musculoskeletal) was generated using full-length Tβ4. The BPC-157 + TB-500 combination is mechanistically rational: BPC-157 builds the local vascular and cellular repair environment; Tβ4/TB-500 contributes systemic cell motility enhancement and, for the full protein, progenitor cell mobilization. Human evidence for the combination does not yet exist.
