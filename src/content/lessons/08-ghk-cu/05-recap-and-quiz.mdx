## The module in one paragraph

GHK-Cu is Glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine chelated to a copper ion (Cu²⁺) — a tripeptide that is genuinely endogenous, released during normal extracellular matrix remodeling from proteolytic cleavage of SPARC. GHK and GHK-Cu are chemically distinct: most documented effects require the copper-chelated form. Its mechanism is unlike the signaling peptides in this course: GHK-Cu doesn't bind a receptor and trigger a cascade. It works by (1) delivering copper safely — chelated so it can't drive free-radical chemistry, but labile enough to be transferred to copper-requiring enzymes — and (2) modulating gene expression broadly. The copper delivery mechanism is most relevant to lysyl oxidase, which crosslinks collagen and elastin; the chain from GHK-Cu to LOX to matrix quality is mechanistically sound but the copper-to-LOX transfer step has not been isolated biochemically (inferred, not directly proven). The gene expression story is real at a bioinformatics level — cell culture work shows broad transcriptional changes — but "resetting the genome" is marketing: the Connectivity Map analysis was done in cancer cell lines at 1 µM in vitro and doesn't demonstrate restoration of youthful transcription patterns. Evidence is strongest for topical use (multiple human RCTs on skin quality); injectable evidence is animal-only with no established human dose; the plasma decline narrative (200→80 ng/mL with age) originates from a single 1973 source and has not been independently replicated.
