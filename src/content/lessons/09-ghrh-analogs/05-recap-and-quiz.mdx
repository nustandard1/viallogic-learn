## The module in one paragraph

Sermorelin, Mod GRF 1-29, CJC-1295 with DAC, and tesamorelin are all GHRH receptor agonists — they bind the same Gs-coupled GPCR on anterior pituitary somatotroph cells, trigger the same cAMP/PKA/CREB cascade, and produce endogenous GH pulses. None of them introduce exogenous GH. The differences between them are entirely pharmacokinetic: half-lives span from ~15 minutes (sermorelin) to ~30 minutes (Mod GRF 1-29) to ~26 minutes (tesamorelin) to ~6–8 days (CJC-1295 with DAC). The longer the half-life, the more continuous the GHRH receptor stimulation — flatter GH profiles, reduced pulsatility, potential receptor desensitization over time. The DAC modification in CJC-1295 is a covalent Michael addition between a maleimide group and albumin's Cys-34 — a permanently bonded half-life extension, not a reversible lipidation. Mod GRF 1-29 is chronically mislabeled as "CJC-1295" in research supply chains; the two have a 7-day vs. 30-minute half-life gap that cannot be distinguished without independent COA verification. Tesamorelin is the only FDA-approved GHRH analog (Egrifta, HIV-associated lipodystrophy, 2010); it has completed Phase 3 RCT data establishing human efficacy and safety — a categorically stronger evidence base than any research-only compound in this category, while still limited to its approved indication and population.
