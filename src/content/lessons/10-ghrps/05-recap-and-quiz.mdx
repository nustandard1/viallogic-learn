## The module in one paragraph

GHRPs — ipamorelin, GHRP-2, GHRP-6, and hexarelin — are synthetic ghrelin mimetics that bind GHS-R1a, a Gq-coupled GPCR on anterior pituitary somatotrophs. Binding activates PLC → IP3 → intracellular calcium spike → rapid exocytosis of stored GH vesicles. This is mechanistically distinct from the Gs/cAMP pathway of GHRH analogs — they converge on the same output (GH release) through independent cellular machinery, which is exactly what makes combining a GHRP with a GHRH analog multiplicatively synergistic rather than redundant. GHS-R1a also partially suppresses somatostatin tone, further amplifying the GHRH analog effect when used in combination. All four GHRPs share the receptor; what differentiates them is how selectively they drive GH release without activating the full ghrelin downstream profile. Ipamorelin: most selective — clean GH pulse, minimal cortisol, minimal prolactin, no appetite stimulation; typical half-life ~2 hours; the default starting point. GHRP-2: stronger GH output, moderate cortisol and prolactin elevation, minimal appetite effect. GHRP-6: similar GH to GHRP-2, significant appetite stimulation via hypothalamic GHS-R1a, moderate-to-high cortisol and prolactin. Hexarelin: most potent acute GH pulse, most cortisol and prolactin elevation, fastest GHS-R1a desensitization (tachyphylaxis within 2–4 weeks), and a second binding site at CD36 on cardiac tissue that produces cardioprotective effects via PI3K/Akt and ERK1/2 — independent of GH release and documented in preclinical models but unproven in human clinical trials.
