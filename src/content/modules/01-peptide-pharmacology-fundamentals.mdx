Every peptide conversation — whether about BPC-157 healing a tendon, semaglutide suppressing appetite, or CJC-1295 driving growth hormone release — rests on the same handful of ideas.

A peptide is a *molecule*. That molecule binds a *receptor*. The receptor triggers a *signaling cascade*. The body then *clears* the molecule.

Everything else — the dosing schedules, the side-effect profiles, the marketing claims — is downstream of those four steps.

## What this module covers

This module is the foundation. By the time you finish, terms like **GPCR**, **half-life**, **biased agonism**, and **DPP-4** should feel ordinary, not technical. You won't memorize compounds here — you'll build the framework you'll use to evaluate every compound that follows.

The six lessons take you through:

- **What a peptide actually is** — and the line between peptide and protein
- **Receptors** — how a molecule that can't enter a cell still tells the cell what to do
- **Signaling cascades** — the amplification that lets nanograms produce real effects
- **Pharmacokinetics** — why peptides aren't pills and how engineering buys half-life
- **Agonism and tolerance** — why "more" is often less
- **Recap and quick check** — vocabulary card and a 6-question retrieval test
