Module 1 gave you the framework: peptide molecule → receptor → cascade → clearance. This module answers the next question every practitioner faces: *how do you actually get that peptide molecule to the receptor?*

The answer isn't always a needle — but it usually is. Understanding why, and knowing exactly what to do when it is, is what separates someone who can discuss a protocol from someone who can execute one.

## What this module covers

Three lessons, one goal: after this, reconstituting a vial and calculating a dose should feel routine.

- **Routes of administration** — sub-Q, IM, intranasal, and when oral actually works (it does for select peptides — including ones you'll use)
- **BAC water and reconstitution** — what you're doing when you dissolve a peptide, and the concentration math that every dose calculation starts from
- **Syringe math** — U-100 insulin syringes, why "units" aren't micrograms, and a full worked example from vial to draw

## How to use this module

Lessons 1 and 2 are mostly conceptual. Lesson 3 has math — follow the worked examples and do the checkpoint yourself before reading the answer. The recap quiz includes two calculation questions. If those feel easy, you're ready for Dr. Harmon.
