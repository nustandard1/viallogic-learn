BPC-157 promotes angiogenesis. TB-500 mobilizes stem cells and accelerates the proliferative phase. GHK-Cu drives collagen synthesis and remodeling. Those descriptions are only useful if you know what angiogenesis, the proliferative phase, and collagen remodeling actually do in healing tissue.

That's what this module is for. By the end, you'll be able to trace any regenerative peptide back to a specific phase of healing and a specific cellular mechanism — and every compound-specific module that follows will feel concrete rather than abstract.

## What this module covers

- **The three phases of healing** — the sequence every injury goes through, what happens in each, and what goes wrong when it's interfered with
- **Angiogenesis and blood supply** — why new blood vessel formation is the rate-limiting step in repair, and what VEGF actually does
- **Fibroblasts and collagen** — the cells and proteins that physically rebuild tissue, and why real structural recovery takes months
- **Acute vs. chronic inflammation** — the most important distinction in regenerative medicine, and why "anti-inflammatory" doesn't always mean "pro-healing"

## How to use this module

No math in this one — it's purely conceptual. Read slowly and connect each lesson to something physical. If you've had a tendon injury, a surgical recovery, or even a stubborn muscle strain, you've been through all three phases. That experience is the frame.
