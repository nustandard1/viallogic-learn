When people talk about "taking GH," they usually mean one of three very different things: exogenous growth hormone (a protein injection), a GHRH analog that stimulates the pituitary to release your own GH, or a GHRP that triggers GH release through a completely different receptor. These are not the same thing. Treating them as interchangeable is one of the most common mistakes in this space.

Understanding why requires understanding the axis itself — the chain of signals that runs from the hypothalamus through the pituitary and into every tissue in the body. Once you have the axis, every compound in Modules 9 and 10 becomes obvious rather than arbitrary.

## What this module covers

- **The GH axis** — the full cascade from hypothalamic signal to anabolic effect, and why GHRH analogs stimulate your own system rather than replacing it
- **GH pulsatility** — why GH is released in bursts, not continuously, and why that pattern matters as much as total output
- **IGF-1** — the actual mediator of most "GH effects," why serum IGF-1 is the practical proxy for axis activity, and how it differs from GH itself
- **GHRPs and the ghrelin receptor** — a completely separate receptor and signaling pathway that is synergistic with GHRH, not redundant with it

## How to use this module

Four lessons, each building on the one before. Lesson 1 lays the anatomical map — if the signaling cascades feel familiar, that's Module 1 doing its job. Lesson 3 on IGF-1 is the one most people find clarifying: the "GH builds muscle" story turns out to be mostly an IGF-1 story, and knowing that changes how you read every protocol claim.

The recap quiz in Lesson 5 includes a question that asks you to trace the full axis from hypothalamus to anabolic effect. If you can do it fluently, you're ready for Dr. Harmon.
