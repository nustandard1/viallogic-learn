Most claims in the peptide space are built on a foundation of animal studies, mechanistic plausibility, and anecdotal reports. None of those are worthless. None of them are clinical proof. Knowing the difference — and being able to articulate it quickly — is one of the most useful skills in this field.

This module is two things: a framework for evaluating research quality, and a practical guide to product quality verification through Certificates of Analysis.

## What this module covers

- **The evidence hierarchy** — why a rat study and an RCT are not the same kind of claim, and what each level actually tells you
- **Reading research critically** — sample size, funding, blinding, publication bias, statistical significance vs clinical significance
- **What a COA actually contains** — and how to tell a real one from a vendor-produced PDF
- **Red flags and verification** — how to look up whether a lab is actually accredited, and what ISO 17025 means

## How to use this module

Three lessons, each short and practical. Lesson 1 builds the hierarchy. Lesson 2 teaches you to read a study abstract without being fooled by the framing. Lesson 3 covers product quality — because it doesn't matter how good the evidence is if the compound isn't what it claims to be.

The Dr. Harmon interview for this module is more applied than previous ones — expect to walk through a specific study claim and a COA scenario.
