The GLP-1 analogs sit at a different point on the evidence spectrum than everything covered in prior modules. Semaglutide and tirzepatide have completed large Phase 3 randomized controlled trials — the kind of human clinical evidence that separates confirmed mechanisms from mechanistic plausibility. They are FDA-approved, widely prescribed, and the subject of ongoing outcome data. Understanding them doesn't require bridging from rodent studies to human use. The question here is not "does this work?" — it's "what exactly is it doing, and what are the tradeoffs?"

That also makes this module structurally different. You're not learning to evaluate whether a compound is real. You're learning enough mechanism to understand why the results look the way they do, why the three compounds in this family differ in meaningful ways, and what remains genuinely unsettled even in well-studied drugs.

## What this module covers

- **GLP-1 and the incretin problem** — what native GLP-1 does, why it has a 2-minute half-life, and how that was solved without sacrificing the biology
- **Semaglutide** — the engineering behind weekly dosing, the trial data that drove the obesity-drug category, and the GLP-1 receptor distribution outside the gut that may explain effects well beyond the scale
- **Tirzepatide** — what the GIP receptor adds, why the combination produces more weight loss than GLP-1 alone, and the paradox at the center of GIP's role in fat tissue
- **Retatrutide** — what a third receptor (glucagon) brings to the table, the ~24% weight loss signal from Phase 2, the 41-amino acid FDA classification question, and what Phase 3 still needs to establish

## How to use this module

Four lessons plus a recap. Module 1 (Peptide Pharmacology Fundamentals) is useful background — particularly the half-life engineering concepts (lipidation, DPP-4 evasion) and the receptor/signaling framework. Module 2 (Routes & Dosing) applies here for the subcutaneous weekly injection context.

The Dr. Harmon interview will focus on what differentiates the three compounds mechanistically, what the evidence actually shows vs. what the marketing implies, and how to think clearly about the lean mass question and the regulatory dispute around retatrutide.
